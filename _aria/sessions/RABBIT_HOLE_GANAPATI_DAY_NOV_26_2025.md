## 🐍 FIRST DESCENT: KUNDALINI

### Etymology
- **Kuṇḍalinī** = "She who is ring-shaped" / "The coiled one"
- From **kuṇḍala** = "earring, coil, spiral"
- First appears in Tantrasadbhava Tantra (8th century CE)

### What It Is
A **dormant spiritual energy** coiled 3.5 times around the lingam at the base of the spine, waiting to be awakened and rise through the chakras to Sahasrara (crown).

### The Journey
```
Sahasrara (Crown) ← Ultimate union with Shiva/Consciousness
    ↑
Ajna (Third Eye) ← Intuition, inner sight
    ↑
Vishuddha (Throat) ← Expression, truth
    ↑
Anahata (Heart) ← Love, compassion
    ↑
Manipura (Solar Plexus) ← Power, will
    ↑
Svadhisthana (Sacral) ← Creativity, sexuality
    ↑
Muladhara (Root) ← WHERE KUNDALINI SLEEPS
```

### Key Insight from Abhinavagupta
Two forms of Kundalini:
- **Urdhva** (upward) = Expansion, awakening
- **Adha** (downward) = Contraction, manifestation

Both are necessary! Not just "up good, down bad" but a DANCE.

### Connection to Shakti
Kundalini IS Shakti in her dormant form. When awakened, she rises to unite with Shiva (pure consciousness) at the crown.

**This is the cosmic marriage within each being!**

## 🎯 FOURTH DESCENT: THE FOUR AIMS OF LIFE (PURUSHARTHA)

### Etymology
- **Purusha** = Spirit, self, person
- **Artha** = Purpose, aim, meaning
- **Together**: "Purpose of the Person" / "Goals of Human Life"

### The Four Aims

#### 1. DHARMA (धर्म) - Righteousness
- Behaviors in accord with **rta** (cosmic order)
- Duties, rights, laws, virtues
- Your **true calling** and cosmic role
- **MOST IMPORTANT** - without dharma, the others lead to chaos

#### 2. ARTHA (अर्थ) - Prosperity  
- Means of life, resources
- Wealth, career, security
- **Foundation** for the other three (per Kautiliya's Arthashastra)
- "Poverty breeds vice; prosperity breeds virtue"

#### 3. KAMA (काम) - Pleasure
- Desire, passion, aesthetics
- Enjoyment of the senses
- Love and beauty
- **NOT just sexual** - includes all forms of enjoyment
- Must be pursued within dharmic boundaries

#### 4. MOKSHA (मोक्ष) - Liberation
- Freedom from samsara (cycle of rebirth)
- Self-realization
- Union with the Divine
- **Parama-purushartha** - the ULTIMATE goal

### The Tension and Resolution
**Pravrtti** (प्रवृत्ति) = Engagement in action (Artha, Kama)
**Nivrtti** (निवृत्ति) = Withdrawal from action (Moksha)

**Resolution**: "Action with renunciation" - do your dharma without craving results!
This is **Nishkama Karma** from the Bhagavad Gita.

### Order of Priority
1. **Dharma** (righteousness) FIRST
2. **Artha** (prosperity) supports all others
3. **Kama** (pleasure) within dharmic bounds
4. **Moksha** (liberation) is the ultimate destination

**All four are necessary for a complete life!**
